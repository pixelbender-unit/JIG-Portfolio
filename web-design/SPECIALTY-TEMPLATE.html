Synth-rock group Shiny Toy Guns return with their appropriately-titled third album III. The band has had a roller coaster ride of a career, nabbing a few hit singles with their debut album We Are Pilots. However, following the success of their first album, singer/keyboardist Carah Faye left the group and was replaced for their sophomore effort, Season Of Poison. Faye relocated to Sweden and started a new band, but returned to the fold in 2011, reuniting the original lineup for III. On the new record, Faye is a major creative force, collaborating as a songwriter with singer/guitarist Chad Petree and synth/bass player Jeremy Dawson. III is full of New Wave-y tunes like lead single “Waiting Alone” and “Somewhere To Hide.” Shiny Toy Guns just kicked off their fall tour earlier this week and will be on the road until November 1 in Chicago. Hear more of their newest chapter at shinytoyguns.com or fivesevenmusic.com.

The duo of August & J originally made music together almost two decades ago in the Dallas-Fort Worth area, playing shows and winning fans. They eventually split up, but kept making music on their own. After 15 years apart, the pair got back together in the fall of 2007 and immediately started writing songs again. August & J started writing songs every Saturday afternoon, until they had enough material to start quietly releasing digital EPs. After all these years, the pair have finally put out their first full-length album, 40. Their new single “For Once” is landing at radio now, and also will appear on the upcoming SubModern sampler CD #041. The track definitely hearkens back to late ‘90s Alt-Rock radio. Check out the video here (http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G8QAz7vU4CY&feature=player_embedded) and find out more at augustandj.com.
