Mariah Carey
Island Def Jam has confirmed the release date of Mariah Carey's upcoming thirteenth studio album for July 23. Her latest single, "#Beautiful" featuring Miguel, has served as a preview for the new album. "#Beautiful" has risen to #17 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. The singer tweeted on Wednesday, "The New Era Begins.. 7/23 #MC723."

J. Cole will be setting up a sneak preview of his upcoming album, Born Sinner, in a new and creative way. He tweeted Wednesday night, "Born Sinner will stream 1 time tomorrow at these exact coordinates ... 8pm EST/ 7pm CT/ 5pm PST," and linked to a website with coordinates in Atlanta, Boston, New York, Chicago, Toronto, Houston, Los Angeles and Cole's native Fayetteville, NC. But before you think about just showing up there, there's a few protocols to go through first: "Please bring 1. iphone [or Android] fully charged, 2. with LSNR app downloaded and enabled, and 3. favorite pair of headphones," he tweeted. It seems that the app will determine your location to allow you to stream the album on your phone.

Pharrell recently admitted that Miley Cyrus' upcoming album that he is producing will not be "that hip-hop heavy." The two have been building a friendship in the studio over the past few months, and Pharrell has had nothing but good things to say about Cyrus. "I love Miley — she's really honestly at the core a good person and it's always cool to work with Southern people," he told MTV News, "Coming from Nashville, she just sort of appreciates things in a different way and it comes through in her performance and her aspiration." He continued, "It's just gonna sound like her and her influences, but it's not that hip-hop heavy. That's not what she does, that's not where she comes from. It's a part of what she loves, which is a very big difference."

Jay-Z, Justin Timberlake, Nas, and Timbaland recently spent some time in-studio together. It's unknown what their plan is, but it's likely that they are doing some sort of collaborative piece. Def Jam SVP of A&R Lenny Santiago posted on Instragram a photo of all four laughing around some studio equipment. The caption reads, "JAY x JT x NAS x TIMBO Rat Pack sh--. For some this is 'Work' ... For us it's just Fun #RocNation.”

Janelle Monáe's upcoming sophomore album, The Electric Lady, is due in September, and it will include guest appearances by Prince and Miguel. "We are great friends, and he is a mentor to me," Monáe told Billboard about her relationship with Prince. "It's a beautiful thing to have a friend -- someone who cares about your career, and wants to see you go far and to push boundaries and shake up the world -- give whatever they possibly can to the cause." She would not say much else about the song with Prince, but Miguel guests on the track "Primetime," which is described as a love duet. Meanwhile, fans have already heard the first single, "Q.U.E.E.N.," featuring Erykah Badu.
